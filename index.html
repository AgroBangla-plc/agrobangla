Our Story & Context

Reviving Abandoned Lands to Transform Lives.

The proposed company aims to modernize Bangladeshi agriculture through a farmer-first approach to digitalization and large-scale coordination tailored to Bangladesh's socioeconomic conditions.

Land Consolidation & Tech

The company would acquire land from villages affected by depopulation due to urbanization, with support from the government. After consolidating larger areas of farmland, modern agricultural machinery, technology, and farming techniques would be introduced to improve productivity and efficiency.

70/30 Ownership Model

Farmers whose land is acquired would collectively own 70% of the company's shares. The company retains 30% for management and growth. At the local level, each farm operates independently with farmers holding 70% voting power.

Centralized Services

Centralized services include a national legal team, weather forecasting, risk management, and centralized purchasing, maintenance, and management of farming equipment allowing farms to benefit from shared resources.

Alternative Employment

Employment is prioritized for families assigning land. For others, the company provides training, investment, and support to help establish small businesses like bakeries, ensuring local economic opportunities.

Direct-to-Consumer & Sustainability

In addition to farming operations, the company would work to create direct farmer-to-consumer food exchange systems, reducing dependence on intermediaries that often increase prices while reducing farmers' earnings. The company would also support sustainable agriculture practices, climate resilience, weather-related risk mitigation, and other services designed to improve long-term agricultural stability and profitability.

"The overall goal is to create a nationwide network of modern, digitally connected farms that remain majority-owned by farmers while benefiting from centralized expertise, resources, and support systems."

Phase 1

Reclaiming Abandoned Land

We identify and consolidate uncultivated, fragmented plots (accounting for nearly 43% of fertile land) to create large-scale, viable agricultural zones.

Phase 2

The 70/30 Distribution Model

70% is distributed to skilled local farmers — ensuring fair livelihoods and stopping urban migration — while we retain 30% for management and tech integration.

Phase 3

Tech-Driven Cultivation

Providing farmers with advanced machinery and modern agricultural knowledge, reducing physical labor and bringing foreign-class agricultural efficiency to Bangladesh.

Phase 4

Centralized Agriculture

Creating a structured, cooperative model to boost national food security. With government collaboration, we target an annual yield increase of up to 67%.
